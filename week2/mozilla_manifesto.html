Mozilla Manifesto

XinYan Chen

The Mozilla Manifesto entails the vision for the global internet to be accesible to the public. The principles highlights the importance of maintaining user privacy, security, and idea of the internet being free and open-source. However, though I believe these principles present great intentions by promoting transparency and collaboration, I think the issue of monetary maintainance and intellectual property may present issues in the long-term.

As time goes on and as technologies expand, I am curious to see where the manifesto will head into, whether it addressed these concerns, or be maintained as is.
